Recent affect of COVID-19

The impact of COVID-19 on human beings is not only in the economy, living environment and health, but also in human behavior. A significant increase in alcohol consumption corresponded with the COVID-19 pandemic, with U.S. sales of alcoholic beverages increasing by 55% compared to the same time in previous years. Because using alcohol suppresses immunity, we hypothesized that alcohol consumption may augment the inflammatory response to SARS-CoV-2 infection and that ethanol (EtOH) may worsen the severity of COVID-19 outcomes through modulation of the inflammatory response in COVID-19 patients. In addition, researchers have found that alcohol not only exacerbates the pathology of COVID-19, but may also trigger long-term complications of Alzheimer's disease and other possible neurodegenerative complications.
